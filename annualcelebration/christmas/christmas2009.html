Christmas in Penzance, Newlyn & Mousehole, 2009

 

Christmas in Penzance

The Christmas lights this year will be switched on by the Bob McCredie and the Mayor on Saturday 28th November starting at 3.45 pm in the wharfside. The light display will be along Market Jew Street, stretching to the top of Causeway Head and originating in the Wharfside Shopping Centre.

Saturday 28th November 2009

  • Christmas Lights Switch on - 3.45pm - Causeway Head
  • Christmas Lights Switch on - 4.15pm - Wharfside Shopping Centre
  • Christmas Lights Switch on & the 'light a life' service - 4.30pm - Humphry Davy Statue

Late night shopping

Late night shopping in Penzance will begin on Thursday 3rd December and continues throughout the month every Thursday night.

The Montol Festival 2009

The winter solstice (Cornish: montol)  is celebrated by Penzance and the surrounding towns and villages yearly in December. The towns people are encouraged to wear masks and fancy dress. Much like the Golowan festival, the celebrations last 10 days, culminating at the weekend with montol eve, lantern parades through the streets and a beacon at the leskudjack castle.

The festival runs from the 14th to the 22nd December in 2009, with Montol Eve on Monday 21st of December. Read More

 

Christmas in Newlyn & Mousehole

  • Newlyn Christmas lights switch on - Friday 11th December at 7.30pm
  • Mousehole Christmas lights switch on - Saturday 12th December and 7.30pm.
    (Roads closed from 5.30)

It is highly recommended that you do not take your car to Newlyn or Mousehole on these nights. The streets will be packed with people, and parking will be near impossible.

Oats Travel & Mounts Bay Coaches will be operating coach tours of the Christmas displays. Get in contact via the travel page.

British International, located on Eastern Green, will be operating 10 minute helicopter flights over Newlyn & Mousehole. This is the best way to see the festive lights. Contact British International Helicopters or the Tourist Information Centre for more info.

Tom Bawcocks Eve

Tom Bawcock's Eve is a festival held on the 23rd of December in Mousehole. The festival is held in celebration and memorial of the efforts of Mousehole resident Tom Bawcock to lift a famine from the village. During this festival Star Gazy pie (a mixed fish, egg and potato pie with protruding fish heads) is eaten .
